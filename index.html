My name is Moyra. I am a mathematician at heart but now I would describe myself as a data scientist, data engineer and machine learning(ML) engineer. This is a field I have a lot of passion for, whether it be working on ML projects for my own curiosity because there are many readily available datasets online or tutoring others as they transition into all things Big Data and passing on what I have learnt thus far on my individual data analytics journey.

From a young age, my artistic side, creativity, and love for maths made me want to be an architect though I ended up doing my bachelor's degree in Electronic and Communications Engineering. Here I was exposed to modules on computer systems, electronic circuits, engineering analysis, digital technologies, digital signal processing & control, microcomputer engineering and software engineering. After obtaining a first-class degree, I wanted to learn more about how electronic-based products play a vital role in our daily lives so I decided i would benefit from PhD in Electronic Engineering. My research was in data analytics in smart healthcare, and this is where my ML journey began.

During my undergraduate I had learnt and worked mainly with C, C++ and MATLAB, however my research degree required me to learn Python programming, so I had to teach myself using a variety of online resource. As I enjoy acquiring more and more knowledge that will build my confidence and skill set in this field, I enrolled in the AWS re/Start program which exposed me to the wide range of services provided by Amazon Web Services. I had a better appreciation of these services when I attended the 2021 AWS Innovate Conference ML/AI Edition, here I got to see how the tools were being used and this inspired and empowered me to accelerate my AI/ML journey and take my skills to the next level.

I am searching for an opportunity where I can apply the knowledge and skills I have on exciting new projects in the Big Data and AI industry. Until then I will continue to strengthen the skills working with datasets I have found mainly on DataHub and Snowflake.
